## Setup — 4 Steps to Run a Mock Round

### Step 1 — Pair Up

- **2 students per pair**
- Ek student **interviewer** bane
- Doosra **candidate** bane
- Round 1 ke baad **switch** karenge

### Step 2 — Set the Rules

- **5 to 7 minutes** per candidate
- Use sirf **Present tenses** + Past for old projects
- **Body language** observe karo
- Phone / book band karke baith jao

### Step 3 — Ask Questions

- Section ke **7 questions** ek-ek karke poocho
- **Natural conversational tone** rakho
- Help **mat** karo — candidate ko khud sochne do
- Notes lo har answer ke

### Step 4 — Give Feedback

- Use **evaluation rubric** (neeche)
- **Honest** but encouraging raho
- 2 cheezein **achi**, 2 cheezein **improve** karne ki
- Phir **roles switch** karo

---

## The 7 Questions to Ask in Sequence

| # | Question | What to Listen For |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | *"Tell me about yourself."* | Intro structure, **tense correctness**, professional vocabulary, confident closing |
| 2 | *"What are you working on currently?"* | **Present Continuous** use ho raha? Simple project explanation? |
| 3 | *"What tools do you use daily?"* | Right preposition? **Present Indefinite**? "I work with / I use" sahi? |
| 4 | *"How long have you been working with Terraform?"* | **Present Perfect Continuous** sahi? **FOR vs SINCE** sahi? |
| 5 | *"Tell me about a past project."* | **STAR method**? Past tense sahi? Specific numbers / impact? |
| 6 | *"How do you handle a failed deployment?"* | Step-by-step structure? **"First... Then... Finally..."**? Calm tone? |
| 7 | *"Do you have any questions for me?"* | 2 to 3 **smart questions** ready? "No" toh bilkul nahi! |

## Evaluation Rubric (3 Areas — Score 1 to 5 each)

### 1. Language

- Sahi **tense** use ho raha?
- **Prepositions** correct hain? (at, for, in, with, on)
- **Professional vocabulary** use ho rahi? (handle, manage, deploy, monitor)
- Sentence framing **clear** hai?
- **Connector words** use ho rahe? (basically, apart from, this means)

### 2. Body Language

- **Eye contact** maintained?
- **Open posture** — slouch nahi?
- Natural **hand movements**? (folded nahi, visible)
- **Voice** — steady, slow, clear? "umm aaa" minimal?
- **Smile** + relaxed face? (stiff ya nervous nahi?)

### 3. Content and Structure

- **Clear introduction** aur closing?
- Past projects mein **STAR method**?
- **Specific examples** diye? (numbers, tools, outcomes)
- Apne aap **smart questions** poochhe?
- Overall **confident closing**? (abruptly nahi rukna)

**Total Score:** 15 (5 each × 3 areas)

| Score | Status |
|---|---|
| 13 to 15 | Interview Ready |
| 9 to 12 | Almost there — practice 2 more rounds |
| 5 to 8 | Foundation needs work — go back to lessons |
| Below 5 | Start from Lesson 01 again |

## The Golden Rule

**Theory se interview nahi crack hoga — bolne se hoga.**

Class mein har student ka **kam se kam 2 mock rounds** zaroor karwao.

**Class Rule:** Galti karna **OK hai** — yahaan galti karoge, real interview mein nahi karoge. **Yahi mock practice ka purpose hai.**

---

## Solo Practice (Akele Karne Ke Liye)

Class mein partner nahi mil raha? Yeh tareeke try karo:

### Method 1 — Phone Recording

1. Phone par video record karo
2. Apne aap se 7 questions poocho (text mein likh ke read karke)
3. Pause karo, fir jawaab do
4. End mein recording dekho — body language + voice judge karo

### Method 2 — Mirror Practice

1. Mirror ke saamne baitho
2. Apne aap se questions poocho
3. Jawaab do — apni eyes pe focus karte hue

### Method 3 — Friend / Family

1. Family member ya friend ko interviewer banao
2. Unhe questions list de do
3. Honest feedback maango

---

## Final Tip

Mock interview mein **galti karna celebrate karo** — woh galti aapko bachayegi real interview mein. Best mock practice woh hai jo aapko **uncomfortable** kare.

Real interview se pehle **kam se kam 5 mock rounds** zaroor karo.
